03/11/2021

it was halloween recently, and i thought about doing something special for the whole day, but instead i just did some gender affirmation during my lunch period.

the night before, i couldn't / didn't want to sleep and instead spent my time browsing ideas for how to make a shit body look alright. obviously this didn't really help my self-esteem, but living doesn't either and i'm still doing it.

either way, i had an aliceavizandum tweet on the mind, especially since i'd googled the oblique reference it made a few days before. the haha funny joke bit was that 2b (from nier automata) is canonically 148.8 kg, which provides some ... interesting implications (the exact content isn't safe for work). but in the same google, i found out that she's also canonically 168 cm (with heels), which is about my height. with that in mind, hoping to get some amount of gratification out of this, i googled some 2b crossplays (which were few and mediocre), and searched the cost of buying a cosplay set on aliexpress (too much). so me doing anything like that was further out of the question.

(digression: i feel really fucking stupid after writing the above lol. feels insanely misogynistic and just weirdly noncy. idk. i think talking about video game characters in an aesthetic sense is really dumb, especially if you've never played the game [like me, in this case], because it comes off as an insanely reddit / 4chan thing to do. whatever.)

the point that i'm trying to make here in a roundabout way is that i thought about doing a 2b cosplay a lot, and that i'm still thinking about it now. it makes me sad in a way, beyond the simple dysphoria component that comes with unrealistic body standards or whatever (this comes off as overly sarcastic, but i'll have to explain my own opinion on standards and being trans at a later date).

for me, there's a certain hauntological aspect to my idea? aspiration? i dunno. i planned to come out early in junior year (like, october early) and then hopefully, maybe, get on the E as soon as possible. obviously that future was cancelled by covid, and how things between me and my parents have gone to shit, and how i'm still not entirely sure that this is really a genuine part of my identity. but that plan haunts me whenever i think about how i could've done a cosplay for halloween: i could've been trans and out for a year at that point, i could've been thin and maybe taller and more able to meet my own expectations, i could've had a great fucking time.

but no. here i am, with misshapen legs and poor weight distribution, closed up and scared to be with my parents whenever trans people are even mentioned, only affirming myself in the most conservative way possible. it makes me sad in a way, thinking about the future that was lost.

but life is transient. perhaps this too will come to pass.
